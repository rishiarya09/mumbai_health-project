why do we exist

The public healthcare system in Mumbai is reeling under the pressure created on account of overpopulation. The onus of healthcare of the general population lies on the tertiary care hospitals. These centres should ideally be prioritized as they carry the actual load of patients. We aim to provide an extension of the government-equivalent of OPD care to slums in particular as they continue to be the least served part of the city.

The Gap We are Filling

Low income groups are forced to choose public healthcare system that have limited time and resources. Slums in particulars are breeding grounds for tropical infectious diseases due to poor sanitation, overcrowding and civic issues. Particular reliance to "quacks" or under-qualified medical practitioners practising in the slums worsens the issue.

Target population : 9 million as per 2016 census
(41.3% of Mumbai population lives in urban slums)
